## Goal

Story 185 widened the handwritten proof surface to three synthetic fixtures and left the remaining real-handwriting gap explicit. This story should add one small reproducible permissively licensed real handwritten fixture, prove it through the maintained generic image-directory and PDF OCR lanes, and update the handwritten eval and truth surfaces only as far as the fresh evidence supports. The point is to replace the last purely synthetic caveat with one honest real-data checkpoint, not to introduce a handwritten-specific runtime seam.

## Out of Scope

- Broad support for messy cursive archives, multilingual handwriting collections, or archive-scale note ingestion
- A new handwritten-only production runtime path unless the real-fixture baseline proves the generic seam is insufficient
- Live-download fixture dependencies or claims that require the Library of Congress or another upstream host to stay online at test time
- Changing the accepted `doc-web` runtime boundary or Dossier handoff model from ADR-002
- Routing or approved-handoff changes outside the already maintained generic image-directory and PDF OCR seams
- Manual transcript cleanup that narrows the product requirement or hides OCR failures

## Approach Evaluation

- **Simplification baseline**: the current maintained generic OCR seam may already clear a small real handwritten fixture without any new runtime logic. Story 179, Story 182, and Story 185 already proved the helper, corpus, and run-ID pattern, so the first move is to widen evidence again, not architecture.
- **AI-only**: a stronger direct multimodal transcription call on the real fixture may outperform the generic OCR path, but it should only be used as a bounded comparison baseline on the same source slice if the maintained seam is weak or ambiguous.
- **Hybrid**: likely the leading candidate. Check in one real fixture, rerun the maintained image-directory and PDF OCR lanes, and compare a stronger direct baseline only if the generic seam shows a real gap.
- **Pure code**: appropriate only for fixture import/normalization, corpus wiring, focused tests, and truth-surface edits. Handwriting recognition itself stays AI-first.
- **Repo constraints / prior decisions**: `spec:1.1` keeps the recipe surface explicit; `spec:2.1` and `spec:2.2` treat OCR as expensive and evidence-driven; `docs/runbooks/golden-build.md` warns against turning benchmark scoping into product policy; Story 185 explicitly left the real-handwriting gap open after the LOC transcript path proved flaky; ADR-002 is unaffected because this stays inside the existing `page_image_v1 -> page_html_v1 -> doc_web_bundle` boundary.
- **Existing patterns to reuse**: `benchmarks/scripts/run_handwritten_notes_eval.py`, `benchmarks/scorers/handwritten_notes_transcription.py`, `benchmarks/golden/handwritten-notes/corpus.json`, `tests/test_handwritten_notes_eval.py`, `tests/test_image_directory_intake_recipe.py`, `tests/test_pdf_intake_recipe.py`, Story 179's baseline-first proof pattern, Story 182's multi-fixture widening pattern, Story 185's real-fixture candidate notes, Story 167's small fixture-plus-proof workflow, and Story 157's maintained scan-entry parity checks.
- **Eval**: the deciding surface is the existing handwritten corpus helper widened to one real fixture, plus fresh `driver.py` runs and manual artifact inspection. If the generic seam is weak or ambiguous on that real fixture, compare one stronger direct handwritten-transcription baseline before proposing any new maintained seam.
